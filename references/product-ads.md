# Product Ad Patterns for Seedance 2.0

Load this when the user is making an ad: UGC, ecommerce, DTC, brand film, product demo, Meta/TikTok ad, or "I want an ad for X."

## CRITICAL — timestamps are mandatory here too

Every template and every example in this file is written as **timestamped beats**. Product ads are NOT an exception to the timestamping rule from SKILL.md. The beats are where pacing, the signature moment, effect stacking, and density contrast live. Do not flatten these into single prose blocks.

A 10-second product ad = 4–7 beats of 1–2s each. A 15-second = 8–12 beats.

## Product-ad-specific rules

1. **Always upload the product photo as @Image1** and assign it a role: `Reference @Image1 for product shape, label text, and material. Keep the product identical across all beats.`

2. **Re-reference @Image1 in every beat where the product is on screen.** Drift is the #1 failure mode — fight it with repetition.

3. **720p is fine for social.** Don't over-engineer resolution.

4. **Aspect ratio defaults:**
   - TikTok / Reels / Stories / Shorts → 9:16
   - Meta / Instagram feed → 1:1 or 4:5
   - YouTube pre-roll / landing page → 16:9

5. **Lighting signatures by ad type:**
   - UGC: `soft natural daylight through a window, slightly imperfect handheld framing`
   - Polished commercial: `three-point studio lighting, soft key from camera-left, warm rim light`
   - Cinematic brand film: `golden hour backlight, volumetric god rays, soft shadow detail`

6. **Every ad needs a signature beat.** The visual moment that makes it memorable — a liquid droplet falling in slow-mo, the product lid opening with a subtle LED pulse, the hand reaching up and catching light. Call it out explicitly.

## The four proven ad formats

### Format 1 — Hand-demo UGC (highest conversion for physical products)

First-person or over-the-shoulder; hands interact with the product. No face-block problems.

**10-second template:**
```
Close-up of a pair of hands on a [surface] in [lighting]. Product from @Image1 on or near the surface.

Shot 1 (0–2s): The hands rest near the product from @Image1, still. Camera: handheld close, subtle natural shake. Low density.
Shot 2 (2–4s): The hands pick up and orient the product from @Image1 toward camera, label legible. Camera: handheld, slight dolly-in 6 inches.
Shot 3 (4–6s): Signature beat — [specific interaction moment: peels wrapper / twists cap / swipes surface]. Stacked effects: shallow rack focus on the interaction point + gentle speed ramp (deceleration) to ~50% speed.
Shot 4 (6–8s): Speed ramps back up. The [result / aftermath of the interaction] shows. Camera: continuing handheld, settles back.
Shot 5 (8–10s): Energy resolves — hands set product from @Image1 back, label toward camera, framing stabilizes, lighting holds. 

Style: iPhone UGC, slightly imperfect framing, natural daylight. Product from @Image1 identical throughout — same shape, label, colors. Smooth handheld motion, stable framing through signature beat, no warping, no identity drift.
```

### Format 2 — Character testimonial (requires stylized character sheet)

Needs `@Image1 = character sheet` (no real-face photos). `@Image2 = product`.

**10-second template:**
```
Medium shot of the character from @Image1 in [environment, lighting]. Product from @Image2 in scene.

Shot 1 (0–2s): Character from @Image1 holds product from @Image2 casually at chest height, noticing it. Camera: locked medium, natural light.
Shot 2 (2–4s): Character from @Image1 brings product from @Image2 closer to frame, label facing camera. Camera: 1-foot dolly-in.
Shot 3 (4–7s): Signature beat — character delivers the single natural line: "[8–10 words]." Stacked effects: focus pull from product to face + subtle camera handheld micro-shake. Lighting unchanged.
Shot 4 (7–9s): Character's expression settles into a genuine reaction (small smile, knowing nod). Camera: holds medium.
Shot 5 (9–10s): Energy resolves — character lowers product from @Image2 beside them, framing stable, natural rest pose.

Style: [commercial / UGC / cinematic] per brief. Character from @Image1 identical throughout — same face, hair, outfit. Product from @Image2 identical — same shape, label. Smooth motion, stable framing, natural skin tones, no identity drift.
```

### Format 3 — Product hero shot / cinematic macro (no human needed)

Pure product cinematography. Simplest to execute. No face restrictions.

**10-second template:**
```
Extreme macro on the [product] from @Image1, [surface], [specific lighting].

Shot 1 (0–2s): Product from @Image1 at rest on surface, stillness, lighting establishes. Camera: locked-off close, 35mm macro feel. Low density.
Shot 2 (2–4s): Product from @Image1 begins subtle motion — [lid opens / droplet forms / element enters]. Camera: slow dolly-in, 6 inches.
Shot 3 (4–6s): Signature beat — [the specific hero moment: droplet hits surface / LED pulses / cap snaps / steam releases]. Stacked effects: shallow rack focus shift + gentle speed ramp (deceleration) to ~40% speed.
Shot 4 (6–8s): Speed ramps back to normal. Product from @Image1 settles into its final pose, label or hero surface facing camera. Camera: continues slow dolly-in, now 1 foot in.
Shot 5 (8–10s): Energy resolves — motion ceases, frame holds on product from @Image1 fully lit and identical to reference. Camera comes to rest. Final beat lingers.

Style: commercial product cinematography, [film stock / lens style], shallow depth of field, [color palette]. Product from @Image1 identical throughout — same shape, proportions, finish. Smooth focus, stable framing, no warping, no flicker, no identity drift.
```

### Format 4 — Transformation / before-after

Three-act structure. Leverages Seedance's multi-shot strength for skincare, cleaning, software UIs.

**12-second template:**
```
[Environment setup with lighting]

Shot 1 (0–2s): The "before" state visible and problematic, product from @Image1 not yet in scene. Camera: wide locked-off, flat lighting emphasizes the problem. Low density.
Shot 2 (2–4s): Product from @Image1 enters frame in a hand or on surface. Camera: cut to medium close, warmer key light starts coming up.
Shot 3 (4–6s): Signature beat — [application / use moment: swipe / spray / tap / pour]. Stacked effects: shallow rack focus on application point + speed ramp (deceleration) to ~30% speed.
Shot 4 (6–8s): Speed ramps back to normal. The transformation begins — [specific visible change].
Shot 5 (8–10s): Wide re-establishing shot showing the "after" state, product from @Image1 visible, same location as Shot 1 but transformed. Camera: locked medium-wide, key light now full and warm.
Shot 6 (10–12s): Energy resolves — product from @Image1 front-and-center, label clean, final beat holds in still calm. 

Style: commercial [before/after], cohesive lighting progression, [color treatment]. Product from @Image1 identical throughout — same shape, label, colors. Smooth transition between before and after, stable framing on the after state, no warping, no flicker.
```

## Handling the "no real faces" constraint

Seedance blocks identifiable real-face photo uploads at the upload stage. Workarounds:

| Approach | How |
|---|---|
| **Hands-only** | Format 1 — no face needed |
| **Obscured face** | Product covers face; hat brim shadow; hair; sunglasses |
| **Back of head / profile** | Specify in prompt; skip face image reference |
| **Silhouette** | Backlit against window or sky |
| **Stylized character sheet** | Generate a non-photorealistic character in Nano Banana / Flux / Midjourney first, use as `@Image1` |
| **Text-only human description** | Skip the image reference for the person; describe in prose |
| **Product-only** | Format 3 — no humans |

## Product consistency techniques

1. **Upload at 2K+ resolution**
2. **Explicit role assignment**: `Reference @Image1 for product shape, label text, colors, material.`
3. **Re-reference @Image1 in every beat** where the product appears — not just once at the top
4. **Lock identity in the constraint tail**: `Product from @Image1 identical throughout — same shape, label, colors.`
5. **Simpler products consistify better** — single logo > multi-panel box with tiny text
6. **Text WILL wobble slightly** — you can't fully prevent this. For hero label shots, inpaint in post if needed.

## UGC voice and styling — what converts

- **Framing**: handheld, slightly off-center, phone-level height
- **Lighting**: soft natural daylight, ideally from a window behind/beside camera
- **Delivery**: "casual friend telling you about a product" — NOT spokesperson
- **Dialogue rhythm**: one natural beat per ~5 seconds. Include a discovery ("wait"), qualifier ("honestly"), or reaction ("oh")
- **Resolution**: 720p adds organic texture — it's a feature
- **Background**: messy-but-lived-in beats pristine

## Full worked examples

### Example: Glass serum bottle — macro hero shot (Format 3)

```
Extreme macro on the glass serum bottle from @Image1, soft matte surface, north-window daylight from camera-left with warm amber refraction through the glass.

Shot 1 (0–2s): The bottle from @Image1 sits still, catching side light, dropper cap closed. Camera: locked-off close, shallow depth of field on the cap. Low density.
Shot 2 (2–4s): The dropper cap begins to lift slowly out of the bottle from @Image1, a thin golden thread of serum clinging underneath. Camera: slow dolly-in, 6 inches.
Shot 3 (4–6s): Signature beat — a single amber droplet beads at the dropper tip and falls, catching the light mid-fall. Stacked effects: shallow rack focus shift from bottle to droplet + speed ramp (deceleration) to ~25% speed during the fall.
Shot 4 (6–8s): Speed ramps back up. The droplet lands on the surface and spreads slowly. Camera: continues slow dolly-in, now 1 foot closer.
Shot 5 (8–10s): Energy resolves — dropper is back over the bottle from @Image1, serum bead rests on the surface, label of @Image1 legible and sharp. Camera comes to rest. Final beat lingers in stillness.

Style: commercial beauty cinematography, 35mm film texture, muted warm palette, shallow depth of field with soft bokeh. Product from @Image1 identical throughout — same shape, label, glass clarity. Smooth focus, stable framing, natural liquid motion, no warping, no flicker.
```

### Example: Wireless earbuds charging case — Apple-grade hero (Format 3, 10s)

```
Extreme close-up on the white wireless earbuds case from @Image1, matte black marble surface, cool three-point studio lighting with a warm rim along the case edge, shallow depth of field.

Shot 1 (0–2s): The case from @Image1 sits closed and still on the marble, subtle surface reflection visible. Camera: locked-off macro, 35mm lens feel. Low density — pure stillness.
Shot 2 (2–4s): The lid of the case from @Image1 begins to open slowly, revealing the inside edge. Camera: slow dolly-in, 6 inches.
Shot 3 (4–6s): Signature beat — both earbuds rise slightly as if magnetically lifted, the green LED indicator pulses once in a soft bloom. Stacked effects: shallow rack focus shift from lid to LED + gentle speed ramp (deceleration) to ~40% speed during the pulse.
Shot 4 (6–8s): Speed ramps back to normal. The earbuds settle into their raised position, lid fully open, LED steady. Camera: continues slow dolly-in, now about 1 foot closer to the case from @Image1.
Shot 5 (8–10s): Energy resolves — everything holds, the case from @Image1 is fully revealed in final hero framing, glossy white finish catching the rim light, label and proportions identical to reference. Camera comes to rest.

Style: commercial product cinematography, Apple-grade minimalism, muted cool palette with warm rim accents, 35mm film texture, shallow depth of field. Product from @Image1 identical throughout — same shape, proportions, glossy white finish. Smooth focus, stable framing, no warping, no flicker, no identity drift.
```

### Example: Stanley tumbler transformation (Format 4)

```
[Pristine modern desk with soft daylight from a left-side window]

Shot 1 (0–2s): The Stanley tumbler from @Image1 sits still on the desk, lid closed, desk objects visible around it. Camera: wide locked-off, flat daylight. Low density.
Shot 2 (2–4s): A hand enters frame from the right and lifts the Stanley from @Image1 off the desk. Camera: cuts to medium close, hand lifts the tumbler toward camera.
Shot 3 (4–6s): Signature beat — as the hand moves the tumbler from @Image1, the desk background dissolves through a seamless environment warp into a mountain trail at golden hour. Stacked effects: environment dissolve + gentle speed ramp (deceleration) to ~50% during the warp.
Shot 4 (6–8s): Speed ramps back up. Mountain trail now fully established, hand places the Stanley from @Image1 on a granite rock, steam rising from the lid. Camera: locked medium.
Shot 5 (8–10s): Wide reveal — full mountain range behind the Stanley from @Image1, golden hour backlight, product hero-framed on the rock. Camera: slow dolly-in to hero the lid.
Shot 6 (10–12s): Energy resolves — motion ceases, Stanley from @Image1 in final hero pose, warm light holds. Final beat lingers on the product identical to reference.

Style: cinematic lifestyle commercial, warm color grade, shallow depth of field. Product from @Image1 identical throughout — same color, same logo, same shape. Smooth environmental transition, stable framing on the after state, consistent product identity, no warping, no flicker.
```
